# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**Runners** — community members who discover virtual, onsite, and hybrid running events, register, pay, submit GPS or screenshot proof of their completed runs, and track their results and certificates. Context: mobile-aware, varying technical confidence, motivated by community participation and personal milestones rather than elite performance.

**Organizers** — event creators and managers who configure event rules, manage registrations, review payment receipts and run proof, communicate with registrants, and publish results. Context: small-to-medium community event organizers who need reliable tooling without enterprise complexity.

Both audiences have equal standing. Neither is a secondary user.

## Product Purpose

HelloRun is a running event management platform that bridges the gap between casual runners and community event organizers. It handles the full lifecycle: event discovery → registration → payment → run proof submission → organizer review → results and certificates. Success looks like a runner finding an event, completing it, getting their result approved, and earning a verifiable certificate — with an organizer managing that entire flow from a clear, purpose-built dashboard.

## Brand Personality

Energetic, community-driven, professional.

Voice: encouraging and direct, like a supportive race director who takes the event seriously without taking themselves too seriously. Tone shifts by surface: warmer on public-facing pages, more precise in operational tools.

Emotional goal: runners should feel celebrated and trusted; organizers should feel capable and in control.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS (Notion / Linear aesthetic)** — grey-heavy, productivity-tool minimalism. Too corporate and cold for a running community platform.
- **Overcrowded social platforms** — Facebook/community-forum noise. HelloRun is focused workflow first; community feel is carried through personality and warmth, not feature sprawl.

## Design Principles

1. **Every finish counts.** No runner is too slow, too casual, or too new. Hierarchy and empty states should affirm participation, not gatekeep it. Celebrate the milestone regardless of pace.

2. **Clarity earns trust.** The proof-submission and review flow is the platform's critical moment — where community goodwill is won or lost. Every status, reason, and next step must be immediately legible. Ambiguity here breaks the product.

3. **Both sides of the finish line.** Runner UX and organizer UX receive equal craft. A beautiful event discovery page paired with a clunky proof-review dashboard is a failed product. Quality is non-negotiable on both sides.

4. **Confident, not corporate.** Professional enough to trust with payments and certificates; warm enough to feel like a running club. Brand energy comes from color, motion, and copy — not from chrome, density, or enterprise visual patterns.

5. **Workflow over decoration.** Runners are here to submit a run. Organizers are here to review it. The interface should get out of the way of the task. Personality lives in transitions, states, and copy — not in visual noise layered over functional surfaces.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. All interactive elements keyboard-navigable. Minimum 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Proof upload flows must work without drag-and-drop fallbacks.
