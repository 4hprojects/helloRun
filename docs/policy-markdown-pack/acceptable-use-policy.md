# HelloRun Acceptable Use Policy

HelloRun is operated by **Henson M. Sagorsor, operating as 4HProjects**. This Acceptable Use Policy explains how runners, organizers, contributors, visitors, and administrators must use HelloRun fairly, safely, and honestly.

This policy supplements the [Terms and Conditions](/terms) and [Community Guidelines](/community-guidelines). The Terms govern the agreement to use HelloRun; the Community Guidelines give additional guidance for respectful participation.

## 1. Scope and shared responsibility

This policy applies to HelloRun accounts, events, registrations, payments, activity submissions, standings, recognition, community publishing, comments, messages, reports, integrations, and technical services.

You are responsible for activity performed through your account and for information or files you submit. Organizers remain responsible for their event-specific rules and decisions, but those decisions must remain consistent with HelloRun's platform safety, privacy, and integrity requirements.

## 2. Accounts, identity, and access

Use accurate account information and protect your credentials. Do not:

- impersonate a runner, organizer, institution, sponsor, administrator, or another person;
- share credentials, access another account without permission, or attempt to bypass authentication;
- create or coordinate multiple accounts to evade restrictions, obtain duplicate benefits, or manipulate participation;
- misrepresent organizer authority, event ownership, approval, sponsorship, or affiliation; or
- continue a restricted activity through another account or person.

If an account appears compromised, HelloRun may restrict access while ownership and security are reviewed.

## 3. Events, registration, payment, and promotions

Event pages, promotions, packages, deadlines, refund statements, and organizer communications must be accurate and not misleading. Organizers must not publish events they are not authorized to operate or make misleading sponsorship or accreditation claims, unsupported safety or prize claims, or promises of guaranteed outcomes.

Do not falsify registrations, reuse or alter payment evidence, reverse or dispute a legitimate payment dishonestly, bypass configured payment review, exploit discounts or referral systems, or use registration data for a purpose unrelated to the event.

Event-specific commercial and approval decisions remain with the relevant organizer, subject to the [Refund and Cancellation Policy](/refund-and-cancellation-policy), [Organiser Terms](/organiser-terms), and applicable law.

## 4. Activity proof, results, and recognition

Submit only eligible activities and evidence that belong to you. Do not:

- alter screenshots or imported records to misrepresent distance, duration, date, identity, or activity type;
- submit another person's activity, duplicate the same activity improperly, or reuse evidence across incompatible entries;
- conceal material information requested for review or attempt to bypass a rejected or closed workflow;
- coordinate accounts or submissions to manipulate ranks, totals, verified activity counts, or public standings; or
- forge, alter, sell, or falsely claim badges, certificates, verification pages, rankings, or other recognition.

Pending activity and payment evidence remains subject to review. Approved accumulated distance may exceed a selected goal, but only approved records affect official progress, standings, badges, or the final certificate snapshot.

## 5. Participant and personal data

Access participant data only for an authorized event or operational purpose. Do not scrape, sell, disclose, repurpose, or combine HelloRun data to profile or identify people without authority.

Never publish another person's contact details, address, payment evidence, identity documents, health information, private messages, account credentials, activity proof, or other restricted records without a lawful basis and appropriate permission. Organizers are responsible for protecting exported participant records and limiting them to the stated event purpose.

The [Privacy Policy](/privacy) and [Data Usage Policy](/data-usage-policy) explain authoritative access, retention, public-result, and data-rights boundaries.

## 6. Community content, messages, and reports

Do not use posts, comments, replies, profiles, event content, messages, or reports to publish or promote:

- threats, stalking, doxxing, harassment, hate, or abusive targeting;
- sexual exploitation, content that endangers children, encouragement of self-harm, or dangerous health advice presented as qualified guidance;
- illegal goods or activity, scams, phishing, malware, spam, or unwanted promotion;
- intellectual-property infringement or copied content presented as your own;
- false accusations, deceptive event claims, or coordinated manipulation; or
- content intended primarily to disrupt discussion or evade moderation.

Do not flood organizers or users with repeated messages, retaliate against someone for reporting a concern, or knowingly submit false or malicious reports. Good-faith disagreement or an unsuccessful report is not itself misuse.

Published community content and revision history may be public. Report evidence and internal moderation information remain restricted as described in the applicable policies.

## 7. Automation, advertising, and platform security

Do not interfere with HelloRun or other users. Without prior written permission, you must not:

- scrape protected or personal data, run automated scans, or reverse engineer restricted systems;
- bypass rate limits, access controls, workflow checks, or API and integration restrictions;
- perform penetration, vulnerability, load, or denial-of-service testing against production systems;
- introduce malware, malicious code, credential-harvesting pages, or harmful links;
- abuse APIs, webhooks, uploads, notifications, or automated requests;
- generate fake registrations, submissions, views, likes, comments, clicks, impressions, or invalid advertising traffic; or
- encourage users to click advertisements or manipulate advertising measurement.

Finding a possible vulnerability does not authorize further testing, access, disclosure, or retention of data. Stop, preserve only the minimum information needed to describe the concern, and use [Contact](/contact) before taking further action. This policy does not grant authorization to test HelloRun systems or create a legal safe harbor.

## 8. Review and proportionate enforcement

When a concern is received, HelloRun may preserve relevant evidence, review available context, consult the responsible organizer where appropriate, and take proportionate action.

Lower-risk first incidents may result in guidance, correction, content removal, a rejected record, or a warning. Repeated or deliberate misuse may result in feature restrictions, removal from an event or standings, organizer-access restrictions, account suspension, or closure.

Serious threats, exposure of personal data, malware, credential abuse, illegal content, or material payment or activity fraud may justify immediate restriction or suspension while review continues. HelloRun may preserve evidence or report conduct when required by law or necessary to protect people and the service.

Where the workflow and safety risk allow it, the affected person may be given a correction path or may ask HelloRun to review the action. This does not guarantee reversal or a particular moderation outcome.

## 9. Reporting concerns and getting help

Use the Report action for an eligible blog post or another runner's comment. Reason-based reports enter the existing moderation workflow; do not include unnecessary personal or sensitive information.

For event, organizer, account, safety, privacy, content, messaging, or security concerns, use [Contact](/contact). Identify the relevant event or page and describe the concern before sending files. Do not email payment proof, activity proof, identity documents, exploit code, or other sensitive records unless an authorized reviewer requests them.

HelloRun is not an emergency service. If someone faces immediate physical danger, contact the appropriate local emergency service or authority.

## 10. Policy changes

HelloRun may update this policy as workflows, risks, or legal obligations change. The current version and effective date appear on this page. Material updates may be announced through a non-blocking in-app notification.

Questions about this policy may be submitted through [Contact](/contact).
