# HelloRun Community Guidelines

HelloRun is operated by **Henson M. Sagorsor, operating as 4HProjects**. These guidelines explain how runners, authors, commenters, organizers, group members, and visitors should participate in HelloRun community spaces.

These guidelines supplement the [Terms and Conditions](/terms), [Acceptable Use Policy](/acceptable-use-policy), [Privacy Policy](/privacy), and [Data Usage Policy](/data-usage-policy). The Terms govern the platform agreement. Acceptable Use addresses fraud, technical abuse, automation, and systemic misuse. Privacy and Data Usage explain how personal information is handled.

## 1. Purpose and scope

HelloRun supports useful, honest, and respectful participation around running events and community publishing. These guidelines apply to:

- profile names, images, and public identity;
- blog posts, comments, replies, and visible revision history;
- running-group names and participation;
- event discussions and organizer announcements;
- participant and organizer messages;
- images, links, and other uploaded media; and
- other public or semi-public contributions made through HelloRun.

Private payment evidence, activity proof, review records, and support communications are not public community posts, but abusive content sent through those workflows may still violate these guidelines and the Acceptable Use Policy.

## 2. Participate with a useful purpose

Keep contributions relevant to the event, article, group, or conversation. Ask genuine questions, share experience, offer constructive context, or raise a concern through the most suitable channel.

Do not repeatedly post the same message, flood organizer contact forms, hijack unrelated discussions, manipulate engagement, or use community features primarily to drive unwanted traffic.

Reasonable promotion may be allowed when relevant and transparent. Disclose sponsorship, affiliate relationships, employment, free products, or another material interest that could affect how readers understand a recommendation.

## 3. Respect people while disagreeing with ideas

Runners may disagree with event decisions, organizer conduct, published advice, results, or another community member. Criticism may be direct and specific when it remains relevant and does not become a personal attack.

Do not:

- harass, intimidate, shame, stalk, or repeatedly target another person;
- use hateful or dehumanizing language based on identity or protected characteristics;
- threaten violence, self-harm, sexual violence, or damage to property;
- celebrate or encourage harm;
- sexually exploit, coerce, or target a person, especially a minor;
- organize pile-ons or retaliation; or
- use reports, replies, or private messages to punish someone for raising a concern in good faith.

HelloRun is not an emergency service. Contact the appropriate local emergency service or authority if someone faces immediate physical danger.

## 4. Protect privacy and sensitive information

Do not publish another person’s private information without appropriate authority or consent. This includes home addresses, private phone numbers or email addresses, account credentials, payment details, government identifiers, private messages, medical records, emergency-contact details, and unredacted evidence files.

Before sharing a screenshot, photo, result, or conversation, remove unnecessary personal information and confirm that you have a lawful and appropriate reason to publish it. A dispute does not justify doxxing or public exposure of private evidence.

Use [Contact](/contact) for privacy concerns. Do not email proof, identity documents, or sensitive records unless an authorized reviewer asks for them through an appropriate channel.

## 5. Be honest about identity, experience, and evidence

Do not impersonate a runner, organizer, sponsor, institution, medical professional, or HelloRun representative. Do not claim qualifications, partnerships, accreditation, event ownership, or first-hand experience you do not have.

Community discussion must not be used to support fake registrations, altered receipts, manipulated activity proof, copied activities, rank manipulation, forged certificates, or other dishonest event participation. Detailed integrity rules are in the [Acceptable Use Policy](/acceptable-use-policy).

Credit sources and obtain permission where required. Do not copy articles, comments, photos, logos, or substantial creative work and present it as your own.

## 6. Share health and training experience safely

You may describe your own training, nutrition, injury, recovery, equipment, or event experience. Make clear when a statement is personal experience rather than professional guidance.

Do not:

- diagnose another person;
- guarantee a health, weight, performance, or recovery outcome;
- encourage someone to ignore serious symptoms or qualified medical advice;
- promote dangerous restriction, overtraining, self-harm, or unsafe substance use; or
- misrepresent professional credentials or unsupported claims as established fact.

Event rules and community posts do not replace advice from an appropriately qualified professional. Urgent health concerns should be directed to an appropriate health or emergency service.

## 7. Keep event concerns constructive

Organizers remain authoritative for event-specific rules, packages, payment decisions, refunds, activity review, schedules, and fulfilment, subject to HelloRun policies and applicable obligations.

Runners may question or criticize those decisions. State what happened, separate facts from opinion, avoid unsupported accusations, and use the event page or [Contact](/contact) when private registration, payment, evidence, or account details are involved.

Organizers must not retaliate against a runner for a good-faith question, correction request, review request, or report. Organizer announcements and replies must be accurate, relevant, and professional.

## 8. Comments, replies, and revision history

Authenticated runners may contribute comments and one-level threaded replies where enabled. The author may edit an eligible active comment during the displayed editing window and within the configured edit limit.

An edited comment may display an Edited marker and public revision history while it remains active. Eligible historical revisions may be redacted by their author, but moderation evidence already preserved with a report remains restricted and intact.

Deleting or moderating a root comment may leave a neutral tombstone when active replies need conversational context. Deleted replies are removed individually. Do not use editing, redaction, or deletion to mislead readers about the substance of an active dispute.

## 9. Reporting community content

Use the reason-based Report action on an eligible blog post, comment, or reply. Select the reason that best describes the content and add only the context needed for review. The report preserves report-time context for moderation integrity. You cannot report your own comment through the normal workflow.

Reports must be made in good faith. Do not coordinate false reports, submit reports to silence disagreement, threaten someone with moderation, or publish private report information.

For profiles, running groups, organizer messages, events, privacy issues, or general safety concerns, use [Contact](/contact). Include the relevant page, event, or reference information, but do not send sensitive evidence unless requested.

## 10. How moderation works

HelloRun may receive a user report, organizer concern, support request, legal request, or platform signal. Relevant evidence may be preserved before the available context is reviewed.

Responses depend on severity, context, repetition, immediate safety, event authority, and available evidence. They may include guidance, a correction request, content removal, report closure, feature restriction, event restriction, account suspension, or another proportionate action.

Automated and assisted signals may help identify content for review, but they do not independently establish a final finding. Where appropriate, HelloRun may provide a correction or review path. Serious threats, privacy exposure, exploitation, malware, illegal content, or material fraud may require immediate restriction.

Editing, redacting, or deleting content does not automatically close an existing report or remove preserved moderation evidence.

## 11. Platform and organizer responsibilities

HelloRun sets platform-wide community, safety, privacy, and integrity standards. Organizers manage their event-specific operations and participant decisions, but their rules cannot authorize harassment, retaliation, privacy exposure, dishonest claims, or other conduct prohibited by HelloRun policy.

HelloRun cannot pre-review every contribution. Community members should use available reporting tools responsibly, and HelloRun may review or restrict content when a concern becomes known.

## 12. Policy changes and contact

These guidelines may be updated as HelloRun’s community features and safety needs develop. The current published version and effective date appear on this page. Material updates may be announced through a non-blocking in-app notification.

Questions or concerns may be directed through [Contact](/contact). Privacy and data requests should follow the privacy contact guidance shown there.
